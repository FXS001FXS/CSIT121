Web of Things (WoT) Discovery

What Is It? Why is it Important? How is it used? Citations

What is it?

In its simplest terms, Web of Things (WoT) Discovery regards the identifying and accessing of Internet of Things devices on a single network enviroment, allowing fluent interaction between their systems. Essentially, this allows internet connectivity with physical devices that would otherwise lack it through standardized web protocols.

As for Discovery in particular, regarding WoT, the main goal is to ensure that intergrated devices automatically identify like devices on a network with little need for manual intervention. This is crucial simply because when upscaling the amount of devices in an environment/ecosystem, there arises a need for systems to be added removed seamessly as needed.

What are Thing Descriptions?

At base value, Thing Descriptions serve to clasify physical devices in a standardized way, breaking them down into three categories.

    - Actions: Capabilities of the device (e.g., Rotate, light on/off)
    - Properties: The device's state of being/settings (e.g., Speed, temperature)
    - Events: Notifications that the device sends when certain triggers are stimulated. (e.g., Keystrokes, mouse-click)
